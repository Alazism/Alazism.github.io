About Me

I am a doctoral candidate in Chemistry with a focus on organic synthesis. My research interests include the development of new synthetic methodologies and their application to the total synthesis of natural products.

Courses

CHEM 112 - Chemical Principles II

My main responsibilities were attending lectures, assisting students during lecture activities, holding office hours, leading Recitations (beginning SP23), making course material (exam questions, review videos, and Recitation worksheets with answer keys), and hosting review sessions before exams. Additionally, I was given the opportunity to lecture on the course while supervised during FA22. During SP23 and FA23, when the instructor was absent, I substituted for them by guest lecturing each section and guiding proctors for the exams.

Examples of course materials

CHEM 310 - Introductory Inorganic Chemistry

My main responsibilities were attending lectures, assisting students during lecture activities, holding office hours, leading Poster Writing workshops, making course material (practice exams and videos), and hosting review sessions before exams. Similarly, I guest lectured the course when the instructor was absent.

Examples of course materials

CHEM 311 - Development of CHEM 411/496W (Inorganic Lab)

My main responsibility was creating and designing a new laboratory course. This included meeting with professors to collaborate and incorporate their ideas, selecting experiments, creating materials (prelabs, schedule, writing assignments, answer keys, instructional guides, and material lists), testing experimentation (synthesis and characterization), working with the stockroom to secure the chemicals and materials needed, creating an independent project with professors using Course-Based Undergraduate Research Experiences, and assisting in the organization and development of the Canvas course page.

Examples of course materials

CHEM 411/496W - Inorganic Compounds and Reactions

My main responsibilities include supervising students through the selected experiments and individual projects, taking detailed observations on student outlook (performance, perception, feeling, and motivation), grading assignments (prelabs and writing assignments), editing assignments based on student feedback, creating rubrics and TA guides for future semesters, modifying Canvas, testing new experiments/synthetic routes, and holding office hours.
